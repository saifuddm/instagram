## Description

Not because kids ruin anything.
But because life changes deeply, beautifully, forever.

And these things are easier before.

1. Go on a trip with no plan.
2. Live a month in another country.
3. Take a road trip.
4. Sleep under the stars.
5. Fly in a hot air balloon.
6. Try an extreme sport together.
7. Disconnect completely no signal, no internet.
8. Go to Burning Man.
9. Party in Ibiza (or your version of it).
10. Take a train to a random city.

11. Have a full week of dates like at the beginning.
12. Write letters to each other for the future.
13. Climb a volcano.
14. Talk openly about fears around parenthood.
15. Live separately for a month.

16. Have a night of honest, deep conversations.
17. Identify and drop toxic relationship habits.
18. Revisit your family values.
19. Spend a full day following your partner’s wishes.
20. Go 24 hours without phones.

21. Build a financial safety cushion.
22. Talk about money properly.
23. Try living on a minimal budget for a month.
24. Learn the basics of investing together.
25. Discuss parenting roles and expectations.

26. Close debts.
27. Decide where you want to live.
28. Find ways to build passive income.
29. Create a family financial plan.
30. Clarify career goals.

31. Start a business (or a project) together.
32. Go to an expensive restaurant just because you can.
33. Start a shared hobby.
34. Buy something you’ve dreamed of.
35. Rewatch movies from your childhood.

36. Spend a day making only spontaneous decisions.
37. Go to a dream concert.
38. Take a cooking class together.
39. Throw a party just for the two of you.
40. Go hiking.

41. Get full health checkups.
42. Declutter your space.
43. Talk deeply about how you want to raise kids.
44. Agree on how family will help (or not).
45. Create family traditions before they’re needed.

46. Choose the place you want to build your life.
47. Write down your family values.
48. Buy the first baby item consciously.
49. Do a meaningful photo shoot together.
50. Enjoy each other while you still have the time, energy and space.

You don’t “lose” life when you have kids.
You just enter a new chapter.

But what you build before…
shapes everything that comes after.".
